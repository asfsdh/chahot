About Me

I am currently pursuing a Master of Engineering (by Research) at Nanyang Technological University, within the College of Computer, Data Sciences (CCDS), under the supervision of Prof. Pranjal Dutta, with a nomination for the National Quantum Scholarship (NQSS). My goal is to deepen my knowledge in quantum information, Fourier analysis, computational complexity, and property testing, preparing for long-term research in quantum computing and quantum security. My current research focuses on quantum computing and theoretical computer science, particularly quantum learning, Fourier analysis of Boolean functions, and property testing. Specifically, I am exploring how quantum-example access and Fourier-sampling procedures can be applied to study learning and testing problems. One of my ongoing questions is whether Fourier samples can help test whether the Fourier mass of a Boolean function is approximately concentrated on a small number of coefficients.
From October 2024 to April 2025, I worked as a Research Assistant at the Centre for Quantum Technologies at the National University of Singapore, within the Department of Computer Science, under the mentorship of Prof. Divesh Aggarwal. My research focused on quantum device authentication and proof of quantumness, which strengthened my interest in the theoretical foundations of quantum information and security. This experience gave me first-hand exposure to the rigor, resources, and collaborative culture of a world-class research environment.
I hold a Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science and Engineering from Seoul National University of Science and Technology, within the Department of Computer Science and Engineering. I also previously pursued PhD studies there under the supervision of Prof. Jong Hyuk Park, working on security for IoT systems, before later leaving the programme as my research interests shifted toward quantum computing and theoretical computer science.
