Motivations

I am passionate about studying and research. However, my previous supervisor always assigned me so much administrative work (It's all his own non-research and non-school related work, with even his family work included) that I did not have enough time to spend on my studies, which was not in line with my original intention of pursuing a PhD. I was eager to improve my research skills in a new environment rather than doing sundry work for my previous supervisor that has nothing to do with my research (minimal pay and no potential for growth). I value the opportunity for personal growth and development and I’m undaunted by the challenges of the academic path. I am confident in my ability to overcome the challenges and to achieve more and build good relationships with new supervisor and fellow students.

After two years of graduate studies, I've come to realize that the essence of academic research lies in the exploration of knowledge, in-depth investigation, and the practical application of innovation. I am eagerly yearning for a more academically challenging environment where research takes precedence over secondary tasks. I believe that in such an environment, I will have the opportunity to fully develop my research skills, employ innovative research methods, and build valuable relationships with like-minded supervisor and peers. I aspire to integrate my research skills with practical application, and I firmly believe that experimentation, simulation, and ultimate implementation are crucial theoretical methods for generating high-quality and highly cited papers. I look forward to pursuing knowledge, creating meaningful research, and actively participating in the international academic community to drive progress in the field.

Overall, I am completely aware that my previous supervisor seldom conceptualizes ideas in research articles and lacks focus on specific research topics, which means I cannot get positive academic guidance from him. I feel that in such an environment, it will be more difficult to acquire the knowledge I seek and work toward my goal. Consequently, I have grown increasingly eager to find a research environment that aligns better with my personal aspirations. Therefore, I quit my previous lab.

Here is a letter of recommendation my previous supervisor had previously written for me, which attests to the fact that even though I was under his tutelage, I still worked very hard and diligently. Despite the fact that my previous supervisor did not provide active academic guidance and often assigned me administrative tasks, I continued to work diligently and earned his recognition.
